# My Notes — Setup Guide

This folder contains your notes app. Follow the steps below to put it online
so reminders work even when the app is not open.

The whole process takes about 10 minutes and costs nothing.

---

## What you need

- A free GitHub account (sign up at github.com if you don't have one)
- This folder of files: index.html, sw.js, manifest.json, icon.png

---

## Step 1 — Create a GitHub account (skip if you already have one)

1. Go to https://github.com
2. Click "Sign up" in the top-right corner
3. Enter your email, choose a password, and pick a username
4. Verify your email address

---

## Step 2 — Create a new repository (a place to store your app)

1. Log in to GitHub
2. Click the green "New" button on the left, or go to https://github.com/new
3. For "Repository name" type:  my-notes
4. Make sure "Public" is selected (required for free hosting)
5. Click "Create repository"

---

## Step 3 — Upload your files

1. On the page that appears after creating the repository, look for the link
   that says "uploading an existing file" — click it
2. Drag all four files into the upload area:
      index.html
      sw.js
      manifest.json
      icon.png
3. Scroll down and click "Commit changes"

---

## Step 4 — Turn on GitHub Pages (free hosting)

1. Click the "Settings" tab near the top of your repository page
2. In the left menu, click "Pages"
3. Under "Branch", change the dropdown from "None" to "main"
4. Click "Save"
5. Wait about 2 minutes
6. Refresh the page — you will see a message:
      "Your site is live at https://YOUR-USERNAME.github.io/my-notes/"
7. Click that link — your app is now live!

---

## Step 5 — Allow notifications in the app

1. Open your app link in Chrome (works best in Chrome or Edge on desktop)
2. You will see a yellow banner at the top asking to turn on reminders
3. Click "Turn on reminders"
4. When the browser asks "Allow notifications?" — click Allow
5. Done! You will now get desktop notifications even when the tab is closed.

---

## Step 6 (optional) — Install as a desktop app

Chrome lets you install the app so it opens like a real program:

1. Open your app link in Chrome
2. Look for a small install icon (looks like a screen with a down arrow)
   in the address bar on the right side
3. Click it and choose "Install"
4. The app now appears in your taskbar / Start menu like any other app

---

## Your app link

After completing Step 4, your link will be:
   https://YOUR-USERNAME.github.io/my-notes/

Replace YOUR-USERNAME with the username you chose on GitHub.

---

## Updating your notes app in future

If you ever receive an updated version of these files:

1. Go to your repository on GitHub: https://github.com/YOUR-USERNAME/my-notes
2. Click on the file you want to update (e.g. index.html)
3. Click the pencil icon (Edit) in the top right of the file
4. Paste in the new content and click "Commit changes"
5. Wait 1-2 minutes and refresh your app link

---

## Troubleshooting

Problem: The app link shows a 404 error
Fix: Wait 5 more minutes — GitHub Pages sometimes takes a little longer the first time

Problem: Notifications are not working
Fix: Make sure you are using Chrome or Edge (not Safari or Firefox)
     Also check that notifications are not blocked in your browser settings:
     Chrome Settings → Privacy and security → Notifications → find your site

Problem: My notes disappeared
Fix: Your notes are saved in the browser you used. If you open the app in a
     different browser or on a different computer, it will start fresh.
     (Cloud sync across devices is not included in this version.)
